“Just Kids” aims to capture how a group of children at the Stay Over Program—an emergency homeless shelter within a public school in San Francisco’s Mission District—spent a small window of their time during breaks from distance learning amid the COVID-19 pandemic.

There are an estimated 52,778 students at San Francisco Unified School District. Among those children, 2,212 have identified as homeless or housing-insecure. This statistic has steadily increased when I first began reporting on the Stay Over Program at Buena Vista Horace Mann K-8 Community School in November 2018 for Mission Local.

As a response to the shelter-in-place order that impacted six Bay Area counties in March 2020 and to meet the needs of the community’s students and families, the Stay Over Program transitioned as an overnight emergency family shelter to running 24/7.

‍

During that early spring, I was immediately charmed with the children at the shelter: their playful new friendships, their mischief-making, their humor, their compassion, and their strength.


I learned about the backstory of a young girl's handmade necklace—gifted to her by a friend from her home country of El Salvador and how it represented their friendship. I witnessed the patience of a teenage boy as he taught a younger boy how to ride a skateboard, as well as a little girl's delight as she proudly displayed her new glittery shoes.


Despite the complicated, unequal political and educational systems they have had to navigate, by placing cameras into their hands, this project was theirs to take ownership of: capturing the ephemeral fleeting moments of their young lives—of kids just being kids.

Here is just a glimpse.
